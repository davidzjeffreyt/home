"Grown Slightly Bald"

In modernism, there is often a focus on consciousness
and self-consciousness. Throughout this poem, the speaker is
concerned with his appearance in society and is insecure
about certain physical attributes such as his baldness. I have
made a video to show that I am also concerned with my
appearance, even to the extent that I am concerned with how
I will look in the future. In The Graduate, Benjamin
Braddock is also concerned with how others view him at first.
While being an exemplary student at college and pleasing his
parents and everyone around him, his appearance in society
is clearly important to him. He initially declines having an
affair with Mrs. Robinson because he does not want to
disrespect Mr. Robinson. Later he hides his affairs from
everyone including his parents to maintain his image.
Even at the Taft hotel, he orders a room for one so people
don’t judge him sleeping with a women and he also gives
a fake name so that if people do find out his affairs, they
won’t know who he really is. Having a reputable image
in society is important to Benjamin at first, but later
disregarded.
