Wept and Prayed

    In The Odyssey and The Penelopiad, Penelope is mentioned to have been praying to Athene for the return of
    Odysseus and for the suitors to leave. One of the qualities associated with Penelope is how she weeps often such
    as when her husband is gone for almost 20 years, or when Telemachus leaves on his dangerous quest. In the poem,
    the speaker says he also weeps and prays to show that he is sad about the problems in his life, and is also looking
    for help/guidance just as Penelope was. In the modern era, it is common for an individual to be lost and as a result
    they will try to find their fit in society and a world with no order. Consequently, the speaker of this poem of weeping
    and praying to deal with this situation. The image I have drawn shows a women (Penelope) praying to represent
    the individuals praying in these texts. I have also incorporated the colour red as a part of the womanâ€™s clothing
    to symbolize the passion the individuals have towards what they are praying for and the importance of these
