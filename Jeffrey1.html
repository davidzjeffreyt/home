The Strength to Force The Moment to its Crisis

It does not take much strength to do things, but it requires a great deal of strength to decide what to do. ~Elbert Hubbard

Elbert Hubbard was an American writer who lived from 1856 to 1915. This is one of his inspiring quotes left behind for us. The speaker in this poem is
uncertain if he has “the strength to force the moment to its crisis” which is referring to the overwhelming question he wishes to ask. This overwhelming
question would result in there having to be a big decision made which is why the speaker refers to it as a “crisis.” The speaker is not sure if he has the
ability to do such a thing, or make such a decision. This quote is straightforward in the sense that it indicates that making decisions is harder than actually
carrying them out. In addition to being applicable to this poem, it can also be applied in the context of Frankenstein when he was faced an immense decision
as to whether or not he should create a female companion for the creature. Despite originally abstaining from doing so, he is convinced to make a female
companion and after some consideration he decides to make one. However, he eventually destroys his latest project and changes is decision. It took a lot of
strength to do that and to be prepared to face the consequences, but the actual action itself is done swiftly.
