I Was Afraid

This video, regarded as a comedic effort, attempts to make the neighbourhood more comfortable by addressing the crimes
that these offenders committed. Living in an area where criminals, especially sex offenders, may exist, can be frightening
to many people. If you don't know the severity of the crime or who these criminals are, you could be living in a state of
fear for the rest of your stay. To be afraid means to be in constant fear. Prufrock exclaims how he is reaching the end of
his journey, where there is someone waiting there, holding his coat, expecting Prufrock to move. The place the Prufrock
will go to, he will likely not return. This instilled fear into him, as he is afraid, like many of us are, of death. In
The Yellow Wall-Paper, the mansion that John and Jane moved into had been empty for years. Jane exlaims how
there was legal trouble between heirs. There were greenhouses but they were all broken. She also directly stated
that she was afraid, and that there was something strange about the house. To live in a place where you are in
constant fear can often times be detrimental to your health, which is evident with Jane in the story.
