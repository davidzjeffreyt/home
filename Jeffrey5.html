Eternal Footman

    This is a picture of Odysseus returning home to fight off his suitors in The Odyssey.
    In this violent scene, death is imminent to either to the suitors or to Odysseus and
    Telemachus as only one group will make it out of the hall alive. The “eternal Footman”
    (or death), is waiting to take some of these lives and this is what Eliot means by the
    eternal Footman holding his coat. In the hall, after Athena intervenes in the battle
    by displaying her sign (the aegis), the suitors realize that Odysseus has supernatural
    assistance. They panic and some even beg for mercy because they know death is
    around the corner.
